# Reviewer Attack List — Anticipated Criticisms and Responses

## Q1: "This is just a computer model. It doesn't prove anything about real biology."
**Response**: We agree. The manuscript explicitly states that results are simulation-consistent under model assumptions, and that empirical behavioral data is required for falsification. The paper's contribution is a mechanistic hypothesis expressed as a computational model, not an empirical claim.

## Q2: "The N3 disease null model fits just as well. HSAP is not distinguishable."
**Response**: This is a key finding, not a flaw. We argue that population trajectories alone are insufficient to distinguish HSAP from disease-like regulation — this is a fundamental identifiability constraint. The paper recommends behavioral and endocrine observables as necessary for empirical testing. See Discussion section 4.3.

## Q3: "B_hsap_abundance doesn't reduce fertility substantially. The model doesn't work."
**Response**: We acknowledge this limitation explicitly in Results (section 3.8) and Limitations (section 5). The current formulation may underrepresent the reproductive restraint mechanism. This is a model limitation that should be addressed in future work with nonlinear fertility functions.

## Q4: "Only 14/50 seeds trigger sink in E. The model is unstable."
**Response**: Sink-triggering depends on stochastic fluctuations in density crossing a threshold. 28% trigger rate in E is consistent with a system near the sink boundary. The 14 seeds that do trigger sink all recover, demonstrating the recovery mechanism's robustness. This variability is biologically realistic — not all populations under identical conditions would experience behavioral sink.

## Q5: "The GA falsification search is circular — you set HSAP-favorable bounds and found HSAP."
**Response**: The falsification search deliberately constrains to HSAP-favorable bounds to test whether alternative attractors exist within the region where HSAP is proposed to operate. Failure to find alternatives suggests HSAP is a robust attractor, not that it is the only possible mechanism. Empirical data is needed to determine which attractor corresponds to biological reality.

## Q6: "Your endocrine model is too simple (3 linear hormones)."
**Response**: Acknowledged in Limitations. The linear model captures the first-order effects of the proposed causal chain. Future work should incorporate nonlinear feedback, receptor dynamics, and individual genetic variation.

## Q7: "No spatial structure or dispersal."
**Response**: Acknowledged in Limitations. The well-mixed assumption is a deliberate simplification to isolate endocrine-behavior mechanisms. Spatial extension is a natural next step.

## Q8: "Where is the comparison to real data?"
**Response**: The paper is a theoretical modeling study. Empirical calibration and validation are the necessary next steps, and the paper provides specific testable predictions to guide that work (Discussion section 4.6).
