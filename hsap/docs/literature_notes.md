# Literature Notes

## Key references

### Calhoun, J.B. (1962)
"Population density and social pathology." *Scientific American* 206:139–148.
- Behavioral sink in confined rat populations
- High-density social breakdown
- Key distinction: crowding-driven pathology vs. HSAP's predator-scarce abundance

### Krebs, C.J. (2002)
"Mammal population regulation, keystone processes and ecosystem dynamics." *Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B* 357:1729–1741. PMID: 14561329
- Bottom-up (food) vs. top-down (predation) regulation framework
- HSAP must clarify vs. existing frameworks, not reinvent

### Stockley, P. & Bro-Jørgensen, J. (2011)
"Female competition and its evolutionary consequences in mammals." *Biological Reviews* 86:341–366. PMID: 20636474
- Female competition for resources and mates
- Supports HSAP's modeling of female aggression as ecologically relevant

### Davidian, E. et al. (2022)
"The eco-evolutionary landscape of power relationships between males and females." *Trends in Ecology & Evolution* 37:706–718. PMID: 35597702
- Male-female power relationships as dynamic and context-dependent
- Supports HSAP's flexible aggression model (not fixed male-aggressive/female-passive)
