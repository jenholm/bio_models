# HSAP Hypothesis

## Core claim

Under low predation, low disease pressure, and abundant resources, mammalian populations can undergo endocrine-linked behavioral shifts that reduce male aggression, alter female competition/aggression, lower fertility, and stabilize population before ecological collapse.

## Causal chain

```
Environmental condition
  → low predation pressure
  → low disease pressure
  → high resource abundance
      ↓
Endocrine proxy shift
  → lower male testosterone proxy
  → altered female testosterone/estrogen balance
  → elevated cortisol under crowding but reduced under abundance
      ↓
Behavioral change
  → reduced male-male aggression
  → increased female resource/offspring defense aggression
  → reduced mating drive at high density
  → increased withdrawal/dispersal
      ↓
Reproductive/population consequence
  → lower fertility / reproductive restraint
  → improved offspring survival per capita
  → population stabilization below carrying capacity
  → reduced probability of overshoot-crash cycles
```

## Key distinction

This is NOT the same as:

- **Calhoun-style crowding pathology**: HSAP separates resource abundance from crowding. Calhoun's behavioral sink is a high-density, high-stress case. HSAP's predicted regulation should appear under low-density, abundant-resource conditions as well.

- **Simple density dependence**: HSAP proposes that endocrine mediation adds predictive power beyond what density alone provides.

- **Food limitation**: HSAP allows resource abundance (food is plentiful) while still producing regulation via social/endocrine mechanisms.

## Non-human focus

This model explicitly does not address human sexual orientation. Where non-reproductive sexual or social behavior is modeled, it is treated as species-specific ethological behavior requiring explicit supporting data.
